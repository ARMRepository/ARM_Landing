What is cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is digital or virtual money that is used as a medium for exchange for various transactional processes. It uses cryptography to secure and verify transactions as well as to control the creation of new units of a particular cryptocurrency.

What are securities offering?

A securities offering ( funding or investment round) is a discrete round of investment, through which a business raises money to fund operations, expansion, a capital project, an acquisition. Aarnav Inc. (“Aarnav”) will be conducting a private placement of securities under Rule 506(c) of Regulation D to the Securities Act of 1933 (the “Act”). Rule 506(c), unlike its counterpart 506(b), permits issuers of securities (i.e., Aarnav) to broadly solicit and generally advertise a securities offering, provided, that:

All purchasers of the securities are “accredited investors” as defined in Rule 501, Aarnav takes reasonable steps to verify purchasers’ accredited investors status, and Certain other conditions in Regulation D are satisfied.

You can raise an unlimited amount of funds through Rule 506(c) – there is no cap.

What is Rule 506 (c) of Regulation D?

Rule 506 of Regulation D provides two distinct exemptions from registration for companies when they offer and sell securities. Companies relying on the Rule 506 exemptions can raise an unlimited amount of money.

What is an ERC20?

ERC20, the Ethereum token standard, is a technical standard used for smart contracts on the Ethereum blockchain. ERC stands for Ethereum Request for Comment.

Who falls under the accredited investor category?

Accredited investors are purchasers who have: (1) an income in the last two years and have expected to make the same amount this year for individuals $200,000 or $300,000 if married, or $1M net worth excluding the value of one’s primary residence.

What is Restricted Securities?

Aarnav will be selling “restricted securities” in the offering, meaning that the securities will bear a “restrictive” legend clearly stating that the purchaser may not sell the security in the public marketplace unless the sale is exempt from the SEC’s registration requirements. Purchasers may utilize Rule 144 under the Act to sell restricted securities, but there are compliance issues, including a six-month or twelve-month holding period, along with sufficient information about Aarnav being in the public domain. Alternatively, purchasers may rely on Section 4(a)(1) for resales.
